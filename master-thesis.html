Master Thesis

This thesis investigates the development and implementation of a monitoring system to effectively map the climate in the barns of Voeders Denys - Decadt. The increasing importance of barn climate to livestock health and welfare is the impetus for this research. The primary goal of this research is to design a robust system to accurately detect negative changes in barn climate so that the livestock farmer can proactively anticipate. A study of potential parameters that harm livestock health is being conducted. Here, temperature, relative humidity, CO2, and NH3 are identified as important parameters.

This is followed by a search for the most appropriate sensors to accurately map the various parameters. The implementation of an IoT system, which combines the appropriate sensors with a suitable communication protocol, is essential to detect negative changes in the barn climate, allowing real-time anticipation. By using a wireless communication protocol, the need for cables disappears, and the system can be attached anywhere in the barn. BLE Coded PHY is used as the communication protocol because of its low power consumption and required distance.
